The Legacy of David Thompson

In Canada and the United States there are many symbols that preserve the accomplishments of David Thompson, even if he didn’t receive such recognition for his efforts during his lifetime, his contributions are well-preserved in some areas today. In Alberta, there is a highway that runs across the province, which bears his name and traverses breathtaking scenery, it is home to many popular hikes and viewpoints scattered about the region, with many hikers, travellers, and aspiring adventurers coining the area as “Dave Thompson Country”. There is no doubt that he once travelled those very lands, when the world was a much more quiet place.

Monuments

There have been monuments established to commemorate his life and achievements. One prominent example is in the community of Lac La Biche, Alberta. According to Tourism Alberta, David Thompson was the first European to land on the shore of the lake which shares its name with the community (Tourism Alberta).

In Canadian Folk Music

The adventurer also receives credit and praise from Canadian folk singer Stan Rogers, who sings of Thompson as someone who carved a path through the rugged terrain of the Canadian wilderness. He sings in thankfulness for the courage of those who explored the unknown, so that he could trace their steps and gain a sense of their adventure: “And through the night, behind the wheel, the mileage clicking west I think upon Mackenzie, David Thompson and the rest Who cracked the mountain ramparts and did show a path for me” (Rogers, 1981).
